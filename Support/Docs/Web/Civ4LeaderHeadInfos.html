Tags

The tags are colour coded depending on whether they are mandatory and must be applied to all entries or whether they are optional with a default value being used if they are not present. New tags are bold.

Text

TagDefaultPurpose

DemandTributeAttitudeThresholdNO_ATTITUDEAn AI won't ask for tribute if their attitude to the recipient is above the recipients value of this tag

TypeThe internal name of this LeaderHead

Integer

TagDefaultPurpose
iAtPeaceAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to be in peace.
iAtPeaceAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast a peace builds up a good attitude toward another leader.
iAtWarAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the bad attitude due to be at war.
iAtWarAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast a war builds up a bad attitude toward an enemy.
iAttackOddsChangeRand0Modifies the Attack odds chance, to make an AI think that a battle is more likely to be won, thus having him more likely to attack (without any real effect on the battle). The total modifier is: iBaseAttackOddsChange + rand(0, iAttackOddsChangeRand) + rand(0, iAttackOddsChangeRand)
iBaseAttackOddsChange0Modifies the Attack odds chance, to make an AI think that a battle is more likely to be won, thus having him more likely to attack (without any real effect on the battle). The total modifier is: iBaseAttackOddsChange + rand(0, iAttackOddsChangeRand) + rand(0, iAttackOddsChangeRand)
iBaseAttitude0The base attitude value the leader has toward everyone else
iBasePeaceWeight0The base value for the Peace Weight, which will be added to a random value (see iPeaceWeightRand). The more the Peace Weights are different between two leaders, the more the two leaders will be unfriendly toward each other.
iBetterRankDifferenceAttitudeChange0Affects how much relations between other leaders are affected by the others to be better in rank.
iBonusTradeAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to trade bonus resources with another leader.
iBonusTradeAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast trading bonus resources with another leader builds up a good attitude toward him.
iBuildUnitProb0Affects how many units an AI will tend to build
iCloseBordersAttitudeChange0Affects how much close borders cause tensions with other leaders.
iConquestVictoryWeight0Weighting this leader places on conquest victories
iCultureVictoryWeight0Weighting this leader places on cultural victories
iDeclareWarTradeRand0Determines how likely an AI leader will try to bring other leaders at war with him against an enemy.
iDefensivePactAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to having a defensive pact with another leader.
iDefensivePactAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast having a defensive pact builds up a good attitude toward another leader.
iDemandRebukedSneakProb0The chance (in percent) an AI leader will start preparing a war against a weaker enemy who denied a request of tribute.
iDemandRebukedWarProb0The chance (in percent) an AI leader will instantly declare war against a weaker enemy who denied a request of tribute. (Note: this tag is not called by the SDK, but by Python: CvDiplomacy)
iDifferentReligionAttitudeChange0The base attitude to have a different state religions than another leader.
iDifferentReligionAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the bad attitude due to have different state religions.
iDifferentReligionAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast two different state religions builds up a bad attitude toward another leader.
iDiplomacyVictoryWeight0Weighting this leader places on diplomatic victories
iDogpileWarRand0A factor that affects how likely an AI leader will start a war to 'dogpile' an enemy (join an existing war against a common victim).
iDominationVictoryWeight0Weighting this leader places on domination victories
iEspionageWeight0Sets how much the AI leader likes espionage (affects both the slider and the overall strategy).
iFavoriteCivicAttitudeChange0The base attitude for adopting the favourite civic of another leader.
iFavoriteCivicAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to adopting the favourite civic of another leader.
iFavoriteCivicAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast adopting another leader's favourite civic builds up a good attitude toward him.
iFreedomAppreciation0The bonus attitude for leaders who have given independence to another leader (when a colony is split).
iLimitedWarPowerRatio0Specifies the minimum power ratio (in percent) an AI leader must have with a leader, to consider him a valid target for a "limited" war.
iLimitedWarRand0A factor that affects how likely an AI leader will start a "limited" war.
iLostWarAttitudeChange0An attitude modifier used during a war, when the AI leader has lost more battles than his enemy.
iMakePeaceRand0Determines how likely an AI leader will try to stop a war he has started.
iMaxGoldPerTurnTradePercent0Affects how much of this AI leader's gold per turn will be available for trade.
iMaxGoldTradePercent0Affects how much of this AI leader's gold will be available for trade.
iMaxWarDistantPowerRatio0Specifies the minimum power ratio (in percent) an AI leader must have with a distant leader, to consider him a valid target for a "total" war.
iMaxWarMinAdjacentLandPercent0Affects how much an adjacent rival can be target for a "total" war (specifying how many "adjacent land" tiles must be between the two empires)
iMaxWarNearbyPowerRatio0Specifies the minimum power ratio (in percent) an AI leader must have with a nearby leader, to consider him a valid target for a "total" war.
iMaxWarRand0A factor that affects how likely an AI leader will start a "total" war.
iNoTechTradeThreshold0An AI leader will stop trading a rival any technology, if he realizes that the rival is becoming too advanced, and this happens when the rival has received via trade a certain number of technologies. This value sets the number of technologies.
iOpenBordersAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to having open borders with another leader.
iOpenBordersAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast having open borders builds up a good attitude toward another leader.
iPeaceWeightRand0The maximum random value for the Peace Weight, which will be added to a base value (see iBasePeaceWeight). The more the Peace Weights are different between two leaders, the more the two leaders will be unfriendly toward each other.
iRazeCityProb0Affects how likely an AI will raze enemy cities.
iRefuseToTalkWarThreshold0A factor that affects how long an AI leader refuses to talk when at war.
iSameReligionAttitudeChange0The base attitude to have the same state religion as another leader.
iSameReligionAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to have the same state religion.
iSameReligionAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast the same state religion builds up a good attitude toward another leader.
iShareWarAttitudeChange0The base attitude for sharing a war with another leader against a common enemy.
iShareWarAttitudeChangeLimit0Sets the maximum value for the good attitude due to sharing a war with another leader against a common enemy.
iShareWarAttitudeDivisor0Determines how fast sharing a war builds up a good attitude toward another leader.
iSpaceVictoryWeight0Weighting this leader places on space victories
iTechTradeKnownPercent0An AI leader will monopoly a technology (refusing to trade it) until the specified percent of rivals have acquired it.
iVassalPowerModifier0Affects how much a leader can resist before he accepts to be vassal of someone else.
iWarmongerRespect0Specifies how much relations are affected by warmonger attitudes. Given two leaders and the minimum value of their WarmongerRespect, this bonus applies to both
iWonderConstructRand0A factor that determines how likely the AI leader will build world wonders
iWorseRankDifferenceAttitudeChange0Affects how much relations between other leaders are affected by the others to be worse in rank.

100 = +1 attitude, -500 = -5 attitude
  <MemoryAttitudePercent>
    <MemoryType></MemoryType>
    <iMemoryAttitudePercent>0</iMemoryAttitudePercent>
  </MemoryAttitudePercent>
MemoryDecaysNONEHow likely is an AI leader to forget past events. The XML value is the denominator of a fractional value. The numorator is randomly generated, between 0 and the XML value. This function is checked for each new turn. If the randomly generated value is 0 that turn, the AI will forget one point for that diplomacy type. This is a binomial distribution.
For example, setting the value to 20 for "MEMORY_MADE_DEMAND_RECENT" will cause the game to generate a value between 0 and 20, and divide it by 20. If it turns out to be 0/20, the AI will forget a one point of diplomacy for the "You made an arrogant demand!
  <MemoryDecay>
    <MemoryType></MemoryType>
    <iMemoryRand>0</iMemoryRand>
  </MemoryDecay>
NoWarAttitudeProbsNONESpecifies the chance an AI leader will reconsider a war plan, based on his attitude toward the potential victim
0 will never reconsider; 100 will always reconsider and never declare
  <NoWarAttitudeProb>
    <AttitudeType></AttitudeType>
    <iNoWarProb>0</iNoWarProb>
  </NoWarAttitudeProb>
TraitsNONEThe list of traits this leader starts with. The possible values are defined in the CivilizationsCIV4TraitInfos.xml file
  <TraitType></TraitType>
UnitAIWeightModifiersNONEModifies the weight for an AI leader to prefer some unit types. The possible values for UnitAIType are defined in the BasicInfosCIV4BasicInfos.xml file, in the section UnitAIInfos
  <UnitAIWeightModifier>
    <UnitAIType></UnitAIType>
    <iWeightModifier>0</iWeightModifier>
  </UnitAIWeightModifier>
